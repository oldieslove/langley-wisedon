New Core Computer System Implementation Approaches

Here's What to Expect March 31 - April 3

Excitement is building at Langley as we approach the implementation of our new core computer system.

The preparation began over a year ago with the selection of a system used by some of the nation's largest credit unions. We will complete the transition on April 3, 2018, resulting in improved member service, streamlined operations and additional capabilities for you. The purpose of this communication is to provide you important dates and information on what changes you can expect to see.

In order to complete the transition, we will temporarily close our branches, online and mobile banking, contact center and telephone banking services. Here is the timetable of events:

Conversion Timeline Service Implications

Saturday
March 31, 2018:

Normal Business Hours

Conversion to new system begins at 3:00pm

After 3:00pm

Shared Branching, Online Banking & Telephone Banking will not be available.

Langley ATMs and Non-Langley ATMS (CU24 & Allpoint networks) will remain available.

Sunday
April 1, 2018

Shared Branching, Online Banking and Telephone Banking will not be available.

Langley ATMs and Non-Langley ATMS (CU24 & Allpoint networks) will remain available.

Monday
April 2, 2018

Branches and Contact Center will be closed.

Shared Branching, Online Banking and Telephone Banking will be restored as quickly as possible on Monday.

Tuesday
April 3, 2018

Branches and Contact Center will open with normal operating hours.

Sign Up For Updates

*Required

Please keep in mind this is Spring Break for some schools and Easter Weekend. Our branches are open on Saturday, March 31st to take care of any banking needs you might have before the holiday. Our staff has been testing, training and practicing for the last several months on the new system, and we appreciate your support and patience as they become comfortable with this new technology. We assure you that we are working diligently to minimize any disruption and our team is dedicated to making the transition to this new system as seamless to you as possible.

What's changing, what's staying the same?

There will be no impact to your Credit Cards and Debit Cards during the conversion process or after the conversion. Should you have any difficulty with card transactions during the conversion weekend, including fraud, please use the phone number provided on the back of your card for assistance.

Your original account or member number will not change although you will see places where it is depicted with additional zeros in front of the number. The names of your accounts also do not change. There will be no need to change your checks or your direct deposit.

There are some changes to Share and Loan IDs that we would like to highlight to help you understand how this might affect you. While we tried to minimize renumbering of individual Shares and Loan IDs, we had to renumber IDs that included a decimal point (i.e. S1.1). Generally, a zero replaced the decimal. On our new system Certificate accounts are considered a Share and are identified with an 'S' in the ID. All Certificate IDs were renumbered to ensure they did not conflict with an existing share account.

Online Banking:

There is no change to your user name and password for Online Banking!
Scheduled Bill pay transactions will process as normal.

Telephone Banking:

There is no change to your PIN for Telephone Banking!
The Telephone Banking prompts and terminology are slightly different. Simply call in as normal, utilizing your member number and current telephone banking PIN and follow the new prompts. Navigation within the new system will provide easy access to your accounts.

We want to keep you informed, help you understand the service impact of the conversion weekend, and reassure you that the transition to this new system is an investment that will allow Langley to continue to provide the service to our members that you expect and deserve. Our goal through the transition will be to minimize service disruptions and bring all service channels back online as quickly as possible.

For the most up-to-date information about our conversion, add or update your email address within Online Banking or opt in to receive updates by completing the form on this page.

As always, thank you for being a valued member of Langley. We know you have choices in financial institutions and we tremendously appreciate your business.

How to Update Your Email Address in Online Banking

After signing into online banking:

Mobile App

1. Tap 'Hamburger' Icon to Expand Menu

2. Tap 'Settings & Alerts'

3. Tap 'User Settings'

4. Tap 'Change Email Address'

5. Enter Email Address, Then Click 'Update'

Website Application

1. Click 'Update'

2. Click 'Change Email Address'

3. Enter Email Address, Then Click 'Update'
